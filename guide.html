Kindle Keyboard (K3) Liberation Guide

From stock to searching Anna's Archive directly on your Kindle. Jailbreak + KOReader + annas.koplugin. Everything via USB, no Amazon involvement.

✓ Is this guide for YOUR device?

This procedure is for the Kindle Keyboard (3rd Gen / "K3") only. It has been walked end-to-end and verified on a real device: model B006 (3G + WiFi, US), firmware 3.4.3 — every step confirmed on hardware and recorded in this repo's FIELD-NOTES.md.

Check your device: on the Kindle, Home → Menu → Settings. Note the serial prefix (first 4 characters) and the firmware version at the bottom of the screen. Then:

  • Serial B006 + firmware 3.4.3 → this is the exact field-tested path. Expect it to match step for step.
  • Serial B006/B008/B00A, firmware 3.0–3.4.3 → supported. The steps are identical; you just pick the file matching your serial (every step lists all three variants, and the firmware split is called out). These variants are fully documented but not yet independently field-tested.
  • Serial does NOT start with B006/B008/B00A → this is not your device. Don't run any of these files. For other Kindles, start at KindleModding.org.

In this guide, "confirmed" means we saw it happen on the B006/3.4.3 unit. Anything not yet observed on hardware is labelled as such, so you always know what's proven vs. expected.

Bricking Risk Assessment

The K3 jailbreak has been used safely by thousands since 2010. Honest breakdown:

  • True brick (unrecoverable)? Essentially zero risk. The K3 has USB serial recovery that can restore even a botched firmware update. You'd have to deliberately destroy the bootloader (nothing here touches it) to truly brick.
  • Soft brick (stuck on boot)? Very low. Only happens with the wrong .bin for your model. Fix: hold power 30+ seconds, or plug into USB, delete the .bin, reboot.
  • Functionality loss? None. Amazon already killed all cloud features. There's nothing left to lose.
  • Reversible? Yes. Factory reset from settings restores stock firmware.

The one real risk: Using the wrong .bin for your variant. Triple-check your serial prefix before every install.

Before You Begin

Do NOT factory reset or wipe the Kindle. The jailbreak installs on top of existing firmware. Your books, settings, and content are unaffected.

Identify your model via Home → Menu → Settings. Check the serial number prefix:

Serial PrefixModel.bin suffix
B006Kindle Keyboard 3G + WiFi (K3G)k3g
B008Kindle Keyboard WiFi-only (K3W)k3w
B00AKindle Keyboard 3G International (K3GB)k3gb

Firmware version is at the bottom of the Settings screen (e.g. Kindle 3.4.3). This guide covers 3.0 through 3.4.3.

You need: USB micro-B cable, a computer, ~30 minutes.

Good news — you don't have to hunt for anything. Every file this guide uses is mirrored, verified, and checksummed in the mirror/ folder of this repository. You can complete the whole process from that folder alone — no forum account, no dead links. Original sources are still linked at each step in case you'd rather grab them fresh. Check integrity with sha256sum -c SHA256SUMS inside mirror/.

1

Jailbreak the Kindle

Download: kindle-jailbreak-0.13.N-r18833.tar.xz — it's a .tar.xz (not a zip) from NiLuJe's mr-public storage, under Legacy/. A verified copy is mirrored in this repo at mirror/niluje/.

  1. Extract (use 7-Zip on Windows; tar.xz may need two rounds). Inside Jailbreak/ find the correct .bin — the version number 0.13.N is part of the filename: 
    Update_jailbreak_0.13.N_k3g_install.bin    B006 (K3 3G US)
Update_jailbreak_0.13.N_k3w_install.bin    B008 (K3 WiFi)
Update_jailbreak_0.13.N_k3gb_install.bin   B00A (K3 3G Intl)
    Firmware matters. The package also ships ..._k3g-3.0-to-3.2_install.bin variants. Those are for FW 3.2 and older ONLY. On FW 3.3.x / 3.4.x (incl. 3.4.3) use the plain file (e.g. Update_jailbreak_0.13.N_k3g_install.bin). Using the wrong one greys out "Update Your Kindle".
  2. This is a fresh jailbreak — ignore the _uninstall.bin files (only needed when re-flashing over an existing jailbreak on a 3.x device).
  3. Connect Kindle via USB.
  4. Delete any existing .bin files or update.bin.tmp.partial on the root.
  5. Copy your .bin to the root of the Kindle drive.
  6. Safely eject.
  7. On Kindle: Home → Menu → Settings → Menu → "Update Your Kindle"
Success: Kindle reboots normally. "Update Your Kindle" may now be greyed out (expected). You may see a brief JailBroken message during reboot.

Verify it took (over USB): reconnect the Kindle as a drive and check the root. Two tells, both confirmed on a real B006/3.4.3 device: (1) the jailbreak .bin is gone (consumed by the update — a rejected one would still be sitting there), and (2) a new linkjail/ folder has appeared on the root (containing bin/, etc/, info.txt). That folder is the jailbreak.

Failed - "Update Your Kindle" greyed out before you try: Wrong .bin for your model/firmware. Recheck serial prefix.
Failed - U006 error: Normal on firmware 2.x (ignore it). On 3.x, wrong .bin.
Failed - stuck on boot screen: Hold power 30+ seconds to force reboot. Plug into USB, delete the .bin, reboot. Device is fine.

Install MKK (MobileRead Kindlet Kit)

Required on K3 (pre-K5 device) to run Java Kindlets like KUAL.

Download: kindle-mkk-20141129-r18833.tar.xz — mirrored at mirror/niluje/ (original: NiLuJe's thread). Your K3 file inside is Update_mkk-20141129-k3g-B006_install.bin (matching your serial; k3w/k3gb for B008/B00A).

  1. Extract with 7-Zip (tar.xz, may need two rounds). Find correct .bin: 
    Update_mkk-20141129-k3g-B006_install.bin   B006
Update_mkk-20141129-k3w-B008_install.bin   B008
Update_mkk-20141129-k3gb-B00A_install.bin  B00A
  2. USB → copy .bin to root → eject → Settings → Menu → "Update Your Kindle"
Success: Reboots normally. No visible change - MKK works silently. Confirmed when KUAL launches in Step 5.
Failed - "Update" greyed out: Wrong .bin, or Step 1 jailbreak didn't take. Redo Step 1.
3

Install Updated Developer Certificates

Original dev certs expired April 17, 2025. Without this, KUAL won't launch. Mandatory.

Download: DevCerts-20250419-KeyStore.zip — mirrored at mirror/devcerts/ (original: a MobileRead t=225030 attachment).

  1. Extract. Find correct .bin — note the K3 files use a hyphen after Update (Update-mkk, not Update_mkk): 
    Update-mkk-20250419-k3g-B006_keystore-install.bin   B006
Update-mkk-20250419-k3w-B008_keystore-install.bin   B008
Update-mkk-20250419-k3gb-B00A_keystore-install.bin  B00A
  2. USB → copy to root → eject → "Update Your Kindle"
Success: Reboots. No visible change. Confirmed when KUAL works in Step 5.
Failed - "permissions expired" later in KUAL: DevCerts didn't install. Make sure you used the keystore variant .bin, not a regular MKK one.
4

Block OTA Updates

Prevents Amazon from pushing updates that undo everything. No download needed.

  1. Connect Kindle via USB.
  2. In the root directory, create a folder named exactly: 
    update.bin.tmp.partial
    That's a folder, not a file. The dots are part of the name.
  3. Safely eject.
Success: Folder exists on root. Verify via USB anytime.
Note: If deleted (e.g. factory reset), redo this. Keep Airplane Mode on when not using WiFi.
5

Install KUAL (App Launcher)

KUAL appears as a "book" in your library. Opening it launches a menu for KOReader and other homebrew.

Download: KUAL-KDK-1.0.azw2 — a single file (no extracting), mirrored at mirror/niluje/KUAL-KDK-1.0.azw2 (NiLuJe's 2025 build; original: NiLuJe's thread).

⚠️ The KUAL must match Step 3's certificates — the #1 K3 trap. KUAL is signed; the Kindle only runs it if the signature matches the certs you installed. This guide installs NiLuJe's DevCerts (Step 3), so use NiLuJe's matching KUAL: mirror/niluje/KUAL-KDK-1.0.azw2 (~131.0 KB, confirmed working on B006/3.4.3). Two look-alikes that FAIL: the 2022 KUAL-v2.7.29 file (~127.8 KB, cert expired 2025-04-17), and KindleModding's KUAL (~131.7 KB) which is signed for the Hotfix path, not DevCerts. Don't mix sources. (Use 1.0, not 2.0.)
  1. USB → copy KUAL-KDK-1.0.azw2 into documents/ on the Kindle.
  2. Eject. Go to Home. You should see KUAL as a book.
  3. Open it.
Success: KUAL opens with a menu interface. This confirms jailbreak + MKK + DevCerts all worked. First real "it came together" moment.
Failed - "This title is not signed by a registered developer": Almost always the wrong KUAL family. Use mirror/niluje/KUAL-KDK-1.0.azw2 (NiLuJe's, ~131.0 KB — matches the DevCerts from Step 3), not an old 2022 copy and not KindleModding's. Replace the file in documents/, restart the Kindle (hold power ~30s), reopen. If it persists: re-apply Step 3 (DevCerts) and reboot twice; then check the Kindle's clock (a wrong date makes valid certs look invalid — connect WiFi a minute to sync it). Last resort, switch to the Hotfix path (mirror/kindlemodding/Update_hotfix_universal.bin + KindleModding's KUAL — same family).
Failed - "application expired" / permissions error: DevCerts (Step 3) didn't install. Redo Step 3.
Failed - not in library: Must be in documents/ not root. Restart Kindle if needed.
Failed - crashes / blank screen: MKK (Step 2) didn't install. Redo Step 2.

Install MRPI (Package Installer)

Lets KUAL discover extensions. Required for KOReader to appear in the menu.

Download: kual-mrinstaller-1.7.N-r18983.tar.xz — mirrored at mirror/niluje/ (original: NiLuJe's thread).

  1. Extract. You get extensions/ and mrpackages/ folders.
  2. USB → copy both to Kindle root. Merge if prompted.
  3. Eject. Open KUAL — should show new MRPI/Helper entries.
Success: KUAL shows MRPI entries.
Space: Needs ~220 MB free. Remove books if tight. Airplane Mode on before freeing space.
7

Install KOReader

The alternative reader. Supports EPUB, PDF, DJVU, MOBI, and dozens more. Has its own modern TLS stack and OPDS browser.

Download: koreader-kindle-legacy-v2026.03.zip — mirrored at mirror/koreader/ (original: KOReader releases). The filename must say "kindle-legacy" — the plain "kindle" build won't run on the K3's old ARM. (Newer KOReader releases work; grab the latest kindle-legacy if you prefer.)

  1. Extract. You get extensions/ and koreader/ folders.
  2. USB → copy both to Kindle root. Merge when prompted.
  3. Eject. Open KUAL → "KOReader" entry should appear.
  4. Select "Start KOReader (no framework)" — kills stock GUI to free RAM. K3 only has 256 MB, this matters.
Success: KOReader launches with a file browser. Looks completely different from stock Kindle. Navigation:
  • D-pad — navigate menus/pages
  • Center/Enter — select
  • Menu key — top menu
  • Back key — go back
  • Physical keyboard — text input
  • Page turn buttons — turn pages
Failed - not in KUAL: extensions/ didn't merge right. Check extensions/koreader/ exists via USB.
Failed - crashes on launch: Wrong package. Must be "kindle-legacy" not regular "kindle". The legacy build targets K3's ARM architecture.
Back to stock: KOReader → Menu → Quit/Exit. "No framework" mode restarts into stock interface. Your normal books are still there.
8

Install Anna's Archive Plugin

Search and download books from Anna's Archive directly on the Kindle.

Download: annas.koplugin-v0.1.8.zip — mirrored at mirror/annas-koplugin/ (original: fischer-hub releases).

  1. Extract. The official release zip already gives you a correctly-named annas.koplugin folder — no rename needed. (If you grabbed source instead and it's annas.koplugin-0.1.8, rename it to annas.koplugin, stripping the version.)
  2. USB → copy into koreader/plugins/ on the Kindle: 
    Kindle root/
  koreader/
    plugins/
      annas.koplugin/    ← here
        main.lua
        ...
  3. Eject. Launch KOReader via KUAL ("no framework").
  4. In the file browser, go to Search menu. "Anna's Archive" should be there.
Success: "Anna's Archive" in search menu. Type a query with the physical keyboard and results appear.
Failed - not in menus: Wrong path or folder name. Must be exactly koreader/plugins/annas.koplugin/main.lua. No version suffix.
Failed - connection error on search: TLS issue. KOReader bundles its own curl but nobody has confirmed this works on kindle-legacy with Anna's Archive specifically. See troubleshooting below.
DNS issues: Plugin has a base URL override setting. Try different WiFi/hotspot if resolution fails.
Setup Complete

The Airplane Workflow

  1. Phone hotspot on (2.4 GHz, WPA2 only — K3 doesn't support 5 GHz or WPA3)
  2. Kindle WiFi on → connect to hotspot
  3. KUAL → KOReader (no framework)
  4. Search → Anna's Archive → type query
  5. Set format to EPUB (recommended for 6" screen — fixed-layout PDFs are painful at 600x800)
  6. Pick result → downloads via LibGen mirrors → open and read

Plugin Settings

  • Download directory — where books save
  • Language filter — 30 languages
  • Format filter — EPUB, MOBI, AZW3, PDF, FB2, DJVU, etc.
  • Sort order — Relevant, Newest, Oldest, Largest, Smallest
  • WiFi off after download — saves battery
  • Base URL override — for when auto-detected domain dies

Plugin auto-detects current Anna's Archive domain via Wikipedia (cached 12h). No account/API key/login needed.

Troubleshooting

Plugin can't connect / TLS errors

KOReader bundles its own curl but this is unconfirmed on kindle-legacy with Anna's Archive.

Fallback: Set up Calibre-Web on your server over plain HTTP and use KOReader's OPDS client. Bypasses TLS entirely.

DNS resolution failures

Anna's Archive domains rotate due to legal pressure. As of June 2026: annas-archive.gl, .pk, .gd.

  • Check on your phone first
  • Use plugin's base URL override
  • Check Wikipedia for current domains
  • Try DNS 1.1.1.1 or 8.8.8.8 on your hotspot

Out of memory / crashes

  • Always use "no framework" launch mode
  • Keep searches specific ("Dune Frank Herbert" not "science fiction")
  • Fall back to OPDS/Calibre-Web if persistent

WiFi won't connect to hotspot

  • 2.4 GHz only (not 5 GHz or "auto")
  • WPA2-PSK (not WPA3)
  • Simple password, no special characters

Downloaded book won't open

  • Corrupted download — re-download (LibGen mirrors can be flaky)
  • DRM-protected files won't work (rare from Anna's Archive)

Does my case with built-in light still work?

Yes. The Amazon Kindle Keyboard case light draws power through the physical hinge connector. It's pure hardware — turns on when cover is open and Kindle is powered. Jailbreak, KOReader, everything here is software only. Light works exactly as before.
